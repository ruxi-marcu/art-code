Semester Project

The application window is separated into two viewports, one the size of the entire window and a smaller one that shows the orthoghraphical projection from above.

The objects are organized into two different hierarchical structures, one of whitch can be moved by keyboard.

The small hierarchy is formed from two stars one of which has been scaled and rotates around the root.

The large hierarchy is formed from 6 stars and is capable of doing multiple transformations: translations, rotations and also combined transformations that give the illusioon of walking.

All the stars have light effects they support ambient , diffuse, specular light and shininess.

Real-Time Rendering

REFLECTANCE

I have created a scene into which I loaded two obj files a teapot and a star. The scene is displayed in 3 different viewports. For every viewport the objects are displayed with a different type of reflectance. The 3 types implemented are, from left to right, Blinn-Phong, Toon, and Cook-Torrance. I have implemented two different light sources, one with blue color and one with pink at different distances from the objects. The animation of the objects can be turned on and off by using the ‘p’ key. The specular, diffuse and shininess parameters can be increased or decreased from the keyboard while the animation in running, using the keys ‘a’,’z’,’s’,’x’ and ‘d’,’c’. The specular and shininess parameters are available only for Blinn-Phong and the diffuse parameter is common for both Blinn-Phong and Toon. For Cook-Torrance none of the 3 parameters will have any effect, since they are all calculated based on the distance from the light sources. For Cook-Torrance the object can be changed to appear either plastic either metallic, also from the keyboard with the keys ‘h’ and ‘n’.>

TRANSMITTANCE EFFECTS

The program uses a cubemap as the environment of the scene. The cubemap uses its own shader.

The transmittance effects: Reflection, Refraction, Chromatic Dispersion and Fresnel, are all implemented in the shader files.

The shader files create the transmittance effects by using the texture from the environment namely the textures of the cubemap.

The scene can be manipulated by the use of the mouse and keyboard allowing movement within it and also the shaders can be switched while the program is running.

BUMP AND NORMAL MAPPING

ASSIGNMENT3 - BUMP AND NORMAL MAPPING The application loads a cubemap as the environment for the scene. Within the scene it loads three different objects from obj files, a humanoid figure, a teapot and a box. For every object the tangents and bitangents are calculated and used to display three different normal textures. The normal textures are loaded by using 2 dds files for diffuse and specular texture and one bmp file for the normal part of the texture. The shaders display the textures on the objects. The scene can be manipulated from the keyboard giving the possibility of movement within the scene and viewing the objects from different angles. The objects can be animated by use of the keyboard. Pressing the ‘p’ key stats and stops the objects rotation. The texture of the objects can be modified from the keyboard allowing us to see the objects with normal mapping and without. It can clearly be seen that there is a huge difference and this difference is emphasized even more when the objects are rotating and the light falls on them in a different way. Libraries The libraries used for making the program are glfw, glew and stb_image, glm.

MICROFACET MODELS

Microfacet Models

Glitter or subtle glints are present in many materials. It applies both to dramatically glittery surfaces like glittery materials snow, craft glitter, metallic crafts and paints, but also to surfaces that present the same shifting in glints without actually being considered glittery. Less obvious glittery surfaces include everyday glossy materials. For example a standard power adapter presents bumps on its surface that create glints that appear and disappear with changes in viewpoints and lights. This kind of materials would appear too smooth in close-up without their specific glints.

Objects that present flakes

The first two outputs focus on representing an object that presents flakes within the composition of its material (Figure 2 and 3). It uses a normal texture that creates some forms on the object’s surface very similar to the surface of an object with crafted glitter on top.

Metallic Paint

The next two shaders give the create a metallic paint type of shader. Metallic paints present multiple layers layers in order to provide the specific effect At least one coating of paint contains microflakes after which a clear coat paint layer is applied.

Snow

Snow and other materials present subtle glints based on the angle of view.

To get the distinct sparkles that can be seen in snow a noise function was added to the texture that perturbs a grid pattern with some noise and with the view vector to let the glittering change based on the angle of view. This noise tries to imitate the specific glints present in snow. The glitter and sparkle effects are added to the final output colour.
