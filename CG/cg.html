Computer Graphics

Semester Project

The application window is separated into two viewports, one the size of the entire window and a smaller one that shows the orthoghraphical projection from above.

The objects are organized into two different hierarchical structures, one of whitch can be moved by keyboard.

The small hierarchy is formed from two stars one of which has been scaled and rotates around the root.

The large hierarchy is formed from 6 stars and is capable of doing multiple transformations: translations, rotations and also combined transformations that give the illusioon of walking.

All the stars have light effects they support ambient , diffuse, specular light and shininess.

Real-Time Rendering

REFLECTANCE

The scene is separated in 3 viewports and each viewport shows a different type of reflactance.

The 3 types implemented are, from left to right, Blinn-Phong, Toon, and Cook-Torrance.

The specular, diffuse and shininess parameters can be increased or decreased from the keyboard.

    Different reflectance models support different parameters:
  • Blinn-Phong - specular, diffuse, shininess
  • Toon - diffuse
  • Cook-Torrance - none of the 3 since they are all calculated based on the distance from the light sources, but supports a different parameter that changes the appearance to either plastic or metallic.
TRANSMITTANCE EFFECTS

The program uses a cubemap as the environment of the scene. The cubemap uses its own shader.

The transmittance effects: Reflection, Refraction, Chromatic Dispersion and Fresnel, are all implemented in the shader files.

The shader files create the transmittance effects by using the texture from the environment namely the textures of the cubemap.

The scene can be manipulated by the use of the mouse and keyboard allowing movement within it and also the shaders can be switched while the program is running.

BUMP AND NORMAL MAPPING

For every object loaded in the scene the tangents and bitangents are calculated and used to display 3 different normal textures.

The normal textures are loaded by using 2 dds files for diffuse and specular texture and one bmp file for the normal part of the texture. The textures are displayed by the shaders.

The scene can be manipulated from the keyboard giving the possibility of movement within the scene and viewing the objects from different angles.

The texture can be modified to show the objects with or without normal mapping in order to emphasize the difference.

MIPMAPS

The application shows the differences between texturing with mipmaps and without.

The program loads an obj file, a plane, and sets on it a checkerboard texture. The plane can be rotated to view the texture from different angles.

The change in texture is more obvious if the plane is very tilted.

MICROFACET MODELS

Glitter or subtle glints are present in many materials. It applies both to dramatically glittery surfaces like glittery materials snow, craft glitter, metallic crafts and paints, but also to surfaces that present the same shifting in glints without actually being considered glittery. Less obvious glittery surfaces include everyday glossy materials. For example a standard power adapter presents bumps on its surface that create glints that appear and disappear with changes in viewpoints and lights. This kind of materials would appear too smooth in close-up without their specific glints.

  • Objects that present flakes - a normal texture is used to create some forms on the object’s surface very similar to the surface of an object with crafted glitter on top.
  • Metallic paints - present multiple layers layers out of which at least one contains microflakes.
  • Snow and other materials - present subtle glints based on the angle of view. To get the distinct sparkles that can be seen in snow a noise function was added to the texture that perturbs a grid pattern with some noise and with the view vector to let the glittering change based on the angle of view. This noise tries to imitate the specific glints present in snow. The glitter and sparkle effects are added to the final output colour.
